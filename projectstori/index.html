Generating Content Ideas (Without the Headache)

Content creation gets easier when you stop staring at a blank page. The trick is to build a system that helps you capture ideas when inspiration strikes. Here’s how:

Know Your Audience:

Who are you creating for? What are their biggest challenges, and how can your expertise make their lives better? Focus your ideas on solving their problems, not just talking about yourself.

Keep an Idea Log:

Use a notebook, a notes app, or a Google Doc to jot down content ideas throughout your day. These could be questions clients ask, quick tips, or lessons you’re learning.

Think in Categories:

Break your expertise into a few key pillars (e.g., “Nutrition Tips,” “Injury Prevention,” “Healthy Habits”) and brainstorm specific content ideas under each.

A Simple Content Framework

Here’s a step-by-step framework to keep your content creation organized and consistent:

  • Define Your Audience: Start by identifying who your customer is and what content will help them most.
  • Brainstorm and Organize Ideas: Jot down ideas daily, then categorize them into formats like videos, short reels, or carousels.
  • Create and Schedule: Dedicate time to filming, editing, and scheduling posts in advance so you always have content ready to go.
  • Rinse and Repeat: Continue collecting ideas, refining your process, and staying consistent.

The goal is to get ahead of your calendar so you’re never scrambling to post.

Example: Jake the Nutritionist

Let’s see how this framework works for Jake, who’s building an Instagram following as he prepares to launch his physiotherapy practice. Jake’s primary audience is busy professionals who want to stay fit and healthy.

While attending class or working out, he thinks of content ideas like “3 Desk Stretches to Avoid Back Pain” or “Quick Healthy Snacks for Busy Days.” He jots these ideas into his phone’s notes app and transfers them to a Google Sheet at the end of the day.

Once a week, Jake picks a few ideas, films quick videos using his phone, and edits them with a simple tool like CapCut. He schedules these posts in Metricool so they’re ready to go, even on his busiest days. As he builds momentum, Jake stays ahead of his calendar and consistently delivers helpful, focused content to his audience.

Introducing: Project Stori

What if content creation wasn’t a daunting task but something that naturally fit into your daily life?

Project Stori is an idea we’re developing to make the content creation process more seamless, intuitive, and even enjoyable.

Our goal is to help you skip the steep learning curve and free up your energy to focus on what matters most:

taking care of your clients and growing your business.

At its core, Project Stori is a Daybook — a simple tool for logging your thoughts, ideas, and experiences.

But it’s more than just a journal. Project Stori will be designed to turn those everyday moments into meaningful, shareable content.

Here’s how it might work:

Jake Using Project Stori

Jake knows his audience: busy professionals who want to stay fit and healthy. Using Project Stori, he creates profiles for up to three customer avatars. These profiles guide his content, ensuring every post addresses his audience’s specific needs.

Throughout the day, Jake uses Project Stori as his Daybook. He jots down ideas from class, like “3 Desk Stretches to Avoid Back Pain,” or records his thoughts on new fitness techniques. When he’s not sure what to write, he taps the “Prompt Me” button for a quick journaling suggestion tailored to his content goals.

At the end of the day, Jake reviews his entries with Project Stori’s AI-powered content assistant. The app pulls potential content ideas from his entries and pairs them with any photos or videos he’s uploaded. If Jake hasn’t added visuals, the app prompts him to find relevant media from his phone.

For the ideas he wants to share, Project Stori generates polished scripts for voiceovers. Jake records his voice, and the app compiles a ready-to-post reel or video with his chosen visuals. Jake reviews the content, makes quick edits if needed, and schedules it—all in one session.

With Project Stori, Jake can effortlessly stay ahead of his content calendar, freeing up more time to focus on building his practice.
